Table of Contents: Lore and Story Structure Document
====================================================

1. Overview
-----------
  1.1 Game Vision
    - Hard sci-fi, multi-generational space opera shaping humanity’s future.
  1.2 Narrative Themes
    - Sacrifice, legacy, competition vs. cooperation.
  1.3 Inspirations
    - For All Mankind, The Expanse, Rimworld, The Sims.

2. Setting
----------
  2.1 The Solar System
    - Realistic depiction, key locations (Earth, Moon, Mars, etc.).
  2.2 Time Period
    - Starts 2040s, spans generations with time skips (20-50 years).
  2.3 Geopolitical Context
    - US-China space race, evolving based on player choices.

3. Story Structure
------------------
  3.1 Chapter System
    - Mission-based chapters, time skips, narrative progression.
  3.2 Macro Story: Space Race
    - US vs. China, war/peace paths, societal outcomes (democracy, communism).
  3.3 Micro Story: Crew
    - Personal arcs, relationships, generational succession.
  3.4 Mission Variety
    - Exploration, colonization, combat/diplomacy (e.g., Mars missile).
  3.5 Endgame
    - Humanity’s solar system fate, crew epilogues.

4. Characters
-------------
  4.1 Crew Overview
    - Small roster (5-10), roles and traits as story drivers (e.g., Alex Chen - Calm Pilot).
  4.2 Crew Arcs
    - Backstories, relationships (e.g., romance leading to offspring), development over chapters.
  4.3 Recruitment Narrative
    - Earth recruits reflecting geopolitics, offspring carrying legacies.
  4.4 Notable NPCs
    - Rival captains, mission control, their narrative roles.

5. Aesthetics
-------------
  5.1 Visual Style
    - Realistic 3D solar system, 2D interiors (US vs. Chinese influence).
  5.2 Audio Design
    - Ship sounds, crew chatter (English/Mandarin), music themes.
  5.3 Evolution Across Chapters
    - Aesthetic shifts based on dominance (e.g., Mandarin labels).
  5.4 Ship Legacy
    - Past ships as museums/wrecks, narrative flavor via logs/cutscenes.

Rationale
---------
- Focus: Purely narrative and lore—setting, story structure, characters, aesthetics, with minimal mechanics beyond what drives the story (e.g., missile as a plot point).
- Structure: Starts with narrative vision, details the world and story, then characters and aesthetics, ending with a sample chapter focused on story beats, not tech specifics.
- Appendices: Includes narrative glossary, inspirations, story questions, and an optional timeline, keeping it creative and world-focused for writers/artists.
