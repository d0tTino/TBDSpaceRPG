## 7.1 Glossary
This glossary defines key narrative and lore-related terms used throughout the document, ensuring clarity and consistency for the creative team.

- **Macro Story:** The overarching narrative focused on the geopolitical competition between the US and China in the space race, shaped by player decisions and mission outcomes.
- **Micro Story:** The personal narratives and relationships of the crew members, driven by their traits, backstories, and interactions.
- **Chapter:** A distinct mission or phase in the game, each with its own objectives, challenges, and narrative arc, contributing to the broader story.
- **Time Skip:** A narrative device where decades pass between chapters, allowing the game to depict generational changes and the long-term impact of player choices.
- **Artemis Coalition:** A fragile alliance led by the United States, bound by the Artemis Accords, which governs lunar and space cooperation but is strained by internal dissent and geopolitical rivalry.
- **Endeavor:** The player’s spacecraft in Chapter 1, a chemically propelled vessel built for the Mars mission, symbolizing humanity’s grit and ingenuity.
- **Tianlong:** The Chinese rival spacecraft in Chapter 1, representing China’s disciplined and state-driven approach to space exploration.
- **Traits:** Personality characteristics (e.g., "Hot-headed," "Genius") that influence crew behavior, task performance, and interactions.
- **Delta-V (Δv):** A measure of the change in velocity required for spacecraft maneuvers, crucial for understanding fuel and trajectory planning.
- **Aerobraking:** A maneuver using a planet’s atmosphere to reduce spacecraft velocity, requiring precise control to avoid catastrophic failure.

## 7.3 Open Story Questions
This section lists areas of the narrative and lore that require further clarification or development. These questions will be addressed in future iterations.

- How will crew traits narratively influence personal arcs and mission outcomes? (e.g., how does a "Hot-headed" trait manifest in dialogue or choices?)
- What specific backstory elements will be tied to each crew role? (e.g., defining the Scientist’s obsession with alien life)
- How will the geopolitical balance shift in response to player choices in early chapters? (e.g., quantifying the impact of the missile decision)
- What narrative mechanisms will be used to track and display crew relationships and generational succession? (e.g., family trees, relationship meters)
- How will notable NPCs like Captain Li Wei evolve across chapters based on player interactions? (e.g., becoming an ally or recurring antagonist)
- What are the specific endgame scenarios for each major narrative path? (e.g., detailing the war or peace outcomes)

## 7.4 Timeline (Optional)
This optional timeline provides a chronological framework for the game’s events, helping to contextualize the narrative progression across chapters.

- **2045:** Chapter 1 - "Race to the Red Planet" - The *Endeavor* and *Tianlong* compete to be the first to land on Mars.
- **2065:** Chapter 2 - "Lunar Accord" - A diplomatic summit on the Moon tests the fragile peace between the US and China.
- **2090:** Chapter 3 - "The Belt Rebellion" - Tensions in the asteroid belt erupt into rebellion, forcing the player to choose sides.
- **2115:** Chapter 4 - "Outer Reaches" - Exploration of Jupiter’s moons reveals new opportunities and dangers.
- **2140:** Chapter 5 - "Endgame" - The final chapter, where the cumulative impact of player choices determines humanity’s fate in the solar system.
