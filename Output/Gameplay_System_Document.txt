3. Crew System
==============

3.1 Crew Design
---------------
Each crew member is defined by three core components: roles, traits, and attributes. These elements determine how crew members perform tasks, interact with each other, and evolve over time.

- Roles: Crew members are assigned specific roles that tie directly to ship systems and tasks. Roles include:
  - Pilot: Responsible for navigation and maneuvering the spacecraft.
  - Engineer: Manages repairs, maintenance, and system upgrades.
  - Scientist: Conducts research and handles scientific equipment.
  - Medic: Maintains crew health and manages medical emergencies.
  - Communications Officer: Handles external communications and diplomacy.

- Traits: Each crew member has one or more personality traits that influence their behavior and task performance. Traits are static and do not change. Examples include:
  - Hot-headed: Increases the likelihood of conflicts but boosts performance under pressure.
  - Genius: Enhances problem-solving, increasing the success rate of complex tasks.
  - Calm: Reduces stress during crises, improving decision-making.
  - Empathetic: Boosts morale of nearby crew members but may reduce efficiency in high-stress situations.

- Attributes: Crew members have dynamic attributes that change based on gameplay:
  - Skills: Proficiency in role-specific tasks, which improves with experience (e.g., successful repairs increase an Engineer's repair skill).
  - Health: Physical condition, affected by injuries or illnesses, which impacts task performance.
  - Morale: Emotional state, influenced by relationships, workload, and mission outcomes. Low morale reduces task efficiency.
  - Relationships: Bonds with other crew members, ranging from friendships to rivalries, which affect cooperation and task outcomes.

Mechanical Impact:
- Task Success: A crew member's skill level, morale, and relevant traits determine the success rate of assigned tasks. For example, a Genius Engineer has a higher chance of successfully repairing a critical system.
- Crisis Resolution: During critical moments (e.g., solar flares), crew members suggest solutions based on their role and traits. The player's choice of solution is influenced by the crew member's status (e.g., a Calm Pilot's suggestion is more reliable).

3.2 Crew Dynamics
------------------
Crew dynamics are driven by relationships and interpersonal interactions, which directly impact task performance and mission outcomes.

- Relationship Types:
  - Friendships: Boost morale and increase cooperation when crew members work together on tasks.
  - Rivalries: Decrease morale and reduce efficiency when rival crew members are assigned to the same task.
  - Romances: Can boost morale but may cause complications (e.g., jealousy) if not managed properly.

- Mechanics:
  - Relationship Meter: Each pair of crew members has a relationship value (e.g., -100 to +100), which evolves based on shared tasks, events, and player interventions.
  - Task Impact: Crew members with positive relationships gain a performance bonus when working together (e.g., +10% task speed). Negative relationships incur penalties (e.g., -15% task success rate).
  - Player Intervention: Players can mediate conflicts or encourage bonds by assigning compatible crew members to tasks or using dialogue options during events.

- Stress and Morale:
  - Stress accumulates during crises or overwork, reducing morale and increasing the likelihood of conflicts.
  - Morale affects overall task efficiency: high morale grants bonuses, low morale incurs penalties.

3.3 Recruitment Mechanics
-------------------------
New crew members are added to the team through two primary methods: recruitment from Earth and offspring from existing crew members.

- Recruitment from Earth:
  - After each chapter, players can select new crew members from a pool of candidates.
  - The candidate pool is influenced by the geopolitical situation:
    - US dominance: Recruits have traits aligned with American training (e.g., Charismatic, Innovative).
    - Chinese dominance: Recruits have traits reflecting Chinese values (e.g., Disciplined, Efficient).
  - Players choose recruits based on their roles, traits, and skills to fit the team's needs.

- Offspring:
  - Crew members in romantic relationships may have children, who join the crew after a time skip (e.g., 20-50 years).
  - Offspring inherit traits from their parents, with a chance for new traits based on a combination (e.g., Hot-headed + Calm = Stubborn).
  - Offspring join as young adults with basic skills, which can be developed over time.

- Integration:
  - New recruits and offspring are integrated into the crew roster at the start of a new chapter.
  - Their traits and relationships are initialized based on their background (Earth recruits) or parentage (offspring).
  - Players must manage the integration process, as new crew members may disrupt existing dynamics.

4. Technical Implementation
===========================

4.1 Game Engine
---------------
The game will be developed using Unity, selected for its robust support for both 2D and 3D game development, extensive community resources, and proven success in similar titles like *Kerbal Space Program* and *Rimworld*. Unity's flexibility enables seamless integration of the game's dual mechanics: 3D orbital navigation and 2D crew management.

- **Key Features:**
  - 3D rendering for the solar system simulation.
  - 2D tools (sprite rendering, tilemaps) for the ship's interior.
  - Physics engine for realistic orbital mechanics (augmented with plugins).
  - Scripting in C# for gameplay logic and AI behavior.

- **Third-Party Plugins:**
  - **Simple Kepler Orbits:** For simulating realistic orbital mechanics.
  - **Yarn Spinner:** For managing dialogue trees and narrative branching.

4.2 Orbital Mechanics
---------------------
Orbital mechanics will be implemented using the Simple Kepler Orbits plugin from the Unity Asset Store, which provides tools for simulating spacecraft trajectories based on Keplerian mechanics.

- **Trajectory Plotting:**
  - Players plot courses using a 3D interface, selecting pre-calculated paths or adjusting parameters (e.g., delta-v, burn timing).
  - Crew members (e.g., Pilot) can suggest course corrections based on new data (e.g., rival ship movements).

- **Propulsion:**
  - Spacecraft use chemical rockets, with realistic fuel consumption and transit times (e.g., 6-9 months to Mars).

- **EVA System:**
  - An extravehicular activity (EVA) feature allows crew to perform tasks outside the ship (e.g., repairs, rescues).
  - Crew are represented as 3D models during EVA, with success rates tied to skills and traits (e.g., Engineering skill, Calm trait).

4.3 Crew Management System
--------------------------
Crew management will be handled in a 2D top-down view of the ship's interior, leveraging Unity's 2D tools.

- **2D Interior View:**
  - Side-facing and back-facing sprites for a cutaway effect, similar to *Barotrauma*.
  - Players can move via WASD or click interactable objects (e.g., computers, panels) for tasks like scanning or comms.

- **Crew AI:**
  - Crew automatically perform tasks based on ship status (e.g., experiments during coasting, strapping in during maneuvers).
  - AI uses state machines or behavior trees to manage behavior, with schedules for routine tasks and event-driven responses.

- **Interpersonal Dynamics:**
  - Relationship system tracks friendships, rivalries, and romances, with a meter (-100 to +100) evolving based on interactions.
  - Failures cause resentment, potentially leading to risky recommendations (e.g., suggesting disliked crew for EVA).

4.4 User Interface
------------------
The user interface (UI) will enable seamless transitions between the 3D space view and the 2D ship interior view, with intuitive controls for navigation and crew management.

- **View Switching:**
  - Players switch between views via UI buttons or hotkeys, with smooth camera transitions.

- **Controls:**
  - Navigation tools for plotting orbits and selecting EVA crew.
  - Crew task assignment via drag-and-drop or schedules.

- **Communications System:**
  - Implements realistic delays based on distance (e.g., 4-20 minutes to Earth, minimal for nearby ships).
  - Dialog trees update over time, with contextual options for character arcs and worldbuilding.

4.5 Data Persistence
--------------------
A save and load system will preserve game state across chapters, ensuring continuity of crew status, ship data, and world state.

- **Save Data:**
  - Crew attributes (health, morale, relationships), ship status, and geopolitical balance.
  - Time skips update crew ages and integrate offspring based on relationships.

- **Implementation:**
  - Use Unity's serialization system (e.g., JSON or binary formats) for efficient saving and loading.

4.6 Localization
----------------
Localization will support multiple languages, particularly English and Mandarin, with dynamic adaptation based on the narrative's geopolitical context.

- **Language Support:**
  - UI elements, dialogue, and audio adapt to the dominant faction (US or China).
  - Use Unity's Localization Package for text and audio management.

4.7 Performance Optimization
----------------------------
Performance optimization is essential to manage the simulation of orbital mechanics, crew interactions, and UI updates.

- **Techniques:**
  - Level of Detail (LOD) for distant planets and ships.
  - Efficient pathfinding for crew movement (e.g., A* algorithm).
  - UI batching to reduce draw calls.

- **Tools:**
  - Unity's Profiler and Frame Debugger for identifying bottlenecks.

4.8 Planet Position Calculation
-------------------------------
Planet positions will be calculated using a simplified orbital model, updated based on the in-game date.

- **Implementation:**
  - Use Keplerian approximations with circular orbits and known periods (e.g., Earth: 365.25 days).
  - Positions are precomputed or calculated once per chapter to minimize runtime impact.

- **Accuracy:**
  - Sufficient for mission planning, focusing on realism without full n-body simulation.

5. Sample Gameplay: Chapter 1 Mechanics
=======================================

5.1 Mission Setup
-----------------
The mission begins with the *Endeavor* in lunar orbit, having arrived via a smaller moon-based lander in the tutorial and prologue. Players must:
- Perform a trans-Mars injection burn to leave lunar orbit and enter a trajectory toward Mars, requiring approximately 1.0 km/s delta-v.
- Plot the trajectory using the 3D navigation interface, selecting an optimal transfer window for fuel efficiency (windows occur every 26 months).
- Manage fuel for chemical propulsion, ensuring enough delta-v for course corrections, Mars orbital insertion, and landing.

Key Mechanics:
- Players use chemical rockets for propulsion, with limited fuel. Transit time to Mars is 6-9 months.
- The ship's systems include life support, power (solar panels), and communications, all requiring crew maintenance.

5.2 Ship Systems
----------------
The *Endeavor* is a chemically propelled spacecraft with modular systems:
- **Propulsion:** Chemical rockets (e.g., liquid oxygen/methane) with finite fuel. Players manage burns for efficiency.
- **Aerobraking Shield and Landing Craft:** The shield is used for Mars atmospheric entry; the attached landing craft detaches after orbit insertion for descent.
- **Crew Module:** Similar to Orion CSM, can undock for separate operations (e.g., docking with a Chinese ship, exploring a low gravity object).
- **Hab Modules:** Remain in orbit for experiments and living quarters, supporting NASA-like scientific tasks.

Mechanics:
- Aerobraking involves managing descent angle and heat buildup via a mini-game or precise timing.
- The crew module can be used for EVA or docking with other ships.
- System failures (e.g., life support) trigger crew repair tasks, with success based on skills and traits.

5.3 Crew Mechanics
------------------
The crew consists of 6 customizable members, each with roles and traits:
- **Customization:** Players select crew traits, backgrounds, and appearances (e.g., faces, hairstyles) at mission start.
- **Roles:** Pilot, Engineer, Scientist, Medic, Communications Officer, and one additional role (e.g., Navigator).
- **Traits:** Examples include Calm, Genius, Hot-headed, Empathetic, affecting task performance and interactions.

Mechanics:
- Crew automatically perform tasks based on ship status (e.g., experiments during transit, strapping in during burns).
- Relationships (friendships, rivalries) evolve, impacting task efficiency and morale.
- Crew suggest solutions during crises (e.g., solar flares), with outcomes tied to their status.

5.4 Objectives and Mechanics
----------------------------
The mission has primary and secondary objectives:
- **Primary Objective:** Land on Mars before the Chinese ship, managing fuel, navigation, and landing.
- **Secondary Objectives:**
  - Conduct scientific experiments (e.g., Martian soil analysis).
  - Maintain crew health and morale.
  - Manage resources (fuel, power, life support).

Crisis Management:
- Events like solar flares or system malfunctions require player decisions.
- Crew suggestions during crises are influenced by traits (e.g., a Calm Pilot suggests safer options).

5.5 Outcome Triggers
--------------------
Outcomes are dynamic, shaped by player choices and crew performance:
- **Success (Optimal):** Land first without conflict, boosting US prestige.
- **Cooperation Path:** Assist a distressed Chinese ship (e.g., docking, rescuing via EVA), potentially leading to joint operations.
- **Conflict Path:** Sabotage the Chinese ship (e.g., using a missile), escalating tensions.
- **Exploration Path:** If the Chinese ship crashes, explore wreckage for survivors, with choices to save or ignore.
- **Failure:** Fail to land (e.g., crash, abort), shifting power to China.

Mechanics:
- Cooperation involves spending delta-v to intercept and dock, with 2D crew tasks (e.g., resource transfer).
- Cascading failures (e.g., navigation error leading to missed burn) require management to prevent further issues.
- Emergent arcs (e.g., saving a crew member's child) are tracked via crew histories and relationship meters.

6.1 Glossary
------------
This glossary defines key technical terms used throughout the Gameplay/System Document to ensure clarity and consistency.

- **Crew Traits:** Static personality characteristics (e.g., "Hot-headed," "Genius") that influence a crew member's behavior, task performance, and interactions with other crew members.
- **Orbital Mechanics:** The simulation of spacecraft movement based on real-world physics, including gravity, velocity, and trajectory calculations.
- **Delta-V (Δv):** A measure of the change in velocity required for spacecraft maneuvers, such as course corrections or orbital insertions.
- **Transfer Window:** A specific period when planets are aligned optimally for interplanetary travel, minimizing fuel consumption.
- **Morale:** A dynamic attribute representing a crew member's emotional state, which affects their task efficiency and likelihood of conflicts.
- **Relationship Meter:** A numerical value (e.g., -100 to +100) that tracks the bond between two crew members, influencing their cooperation and task performance.
- **EVA (Extravehicular Activity):** A feature allowing crew members to perform tasks outside the spacecraft, such as repairs or rescues, with success tied to their skills and traits.
- **Aerobraking:** A maneuver using a planet's atmosphere to reduce spacecraft velocity, requiring precise control to avoid damage.
- **Cascading Failures:** A series of system or task failures that compound over time if not addressed, potentially leading to mission-critical issues.
