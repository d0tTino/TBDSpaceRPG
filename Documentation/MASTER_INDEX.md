### August 2025
- **Knowledge Transmission Dynamics in Isolated Space Settlements**: Added comprehensive research on how knowledge is transmitted across multiple generations (5+ generations/125+ years) in isolated space settlements. Research covers explicit vs. tacit knowledge retention rates, impact of communication latency (0-1 hour) on knowledge transfer, preservation strategies (digital archives, AI mentors, apprenticeships, simulation training), and critical thresholds for irreversible knowledge loss. Updated III_Sociology_Politics_Economics.txt (new Section 1.4.4), II_Human_Factors_Space.txt (Section 2.2), IV_Future_Technologies_Engineering.txt (Section 7.2), and VI_UI_UX_Simulation_Design.txt (new Section 2.3.1) with integrated cross-references.

- **Space Epidemiology in Closed Habitats**: Added comprehensive research on epidemiological dynamics in isolated, closed-loop space habitats ranging from small stations (50 people) to large settlements (10,000+). Research covers pathogen introduction vectors, mutation rates influenced by radiation, transmission models with R₀ values, immune system adaptation to spaceflight, quarantine strategies with effectiveness metrics, diagnostic challenges, and bioregenerative system risks. Updated II_Human_Factors_Space.txt (new Section 3.6), IV_Future_Technologies_Engineering.txt (Section 2.2), III_Sociology_Politics_Economics.txt (Section 1.3), and VI_UI_UX_Simulation_Design.txt (new Section 3.5) with cross-references for an integrated approach to disease management in space habitats.

- **Uncertainty Visualization for Space Simulation Data**: Added taxonomy and cognitive effectiveness analysis of uncertainty visualization techniques for space simulation contexts. Research covers graphical annotations, distributional visualizations, color encoding, interactive techniques, and ensemble visualizations, with detailed metrics on comprehension accuracy (70-95%), decision speed (2-15 seconds), and trust calibration (50-90%) across user expertise levels. Applications include trajectory predictions, resource forecasts, system health prognoses, and AI confidence levels. Updated VI_UI_UX_Simulation_Design.txt (Section 1.2), I_Astrophysics_Orbital_Mechanics.txt (Section 5.5.3), IV_Future_Technologies_Engineering.txt (Section 5.1), and V_Space_Combat_Strategy.txt (Section 5.3) with integrated cross-references.

- **Human-AI Cognitive Integration in Space Operations**: Added comprehensive research on hybrid cognition models for human-AI teaming in space environments, with focus on shared situational awareness under varying communication latencies (internal: <10 seconds; external: minutes to hours) and trust calibration dynamics in long-duration missions. Research includes quantitative models for error rates under stress and latency conditions, trust-performance feedback loops with stability analysis, and critical failure modes in hybrid cognitive systems. Updated II_Human_Factors_Space.txt (Section 2.3), IV_Future_Technologies_Engineering.txt (Section 5.1), and VI_UI_UX_Simulation_Design.txt (Section 4.2) with integrated content on cognitive models, performance implications, and interface design requirements.

- **Faction Lifecycle Modeling in Space Settlements**: Added comprehensive multi-generational (50-200 year) lifecycle model for faction development in space settlements, defining distinct phases (Emergence: 0-20 years, Consolidation: 20-60 years, Maturity: 60-100+ years) with quantitative transition metrics and stability indicators. Research includes computational simulation parameters, AI integration effects, Black Swan event impacts, and generational dynamics influencing faction evolution. Updated III_Sociology_Politics_Economics.txt (Section 2.5), II_Human_Factors_Space.txt (Section 2.3), IV_Future_Technologies_Engineering.txt (Section 5.1.4), and TBD_Space_Game_Research_Foundations.txt (Section 3.5) with cross-referenced content on governance transitions, technology effects, and stability metrics for game implementation.

- **Multi-Planetary Human Psychology**: Added comprehensive research on the psychological and sociological effects of multi-planetary human existence, including frameworks for existential perspective evolution, species identity bifurcation, adaptive risk perception, Earth relationship changes, and societal divergence drivers. Research includes detailed analysis of solastalgia in first-generation settlers, generational attachment decline to Earth, cosmic identity formation, and quantitative models of cultural divergence between settlements. Updated II_Human_Factors_Space.txt (Section 2.6) and III_Sociology_Politics_Economics.txt (new Section 1.4.5) with integrated cross-references examining both individual psychological and broader sociological implications for the development of multi-planetary humanity.

Research includes detailed neurobiological mechanisms (Default Mode Network changes, neural correlates of awe, vestibular system impacts), manifestation conditions, cognitive and behavioral impacts, and adaptive vs. maladaptive expressions. ### April 2025
- **Space Combat Collateral Damage Modeling Research** - Comprehensive analysis of collateral damage to civilian space infrastructure has been integrated into V_Space_Combat_Strategy.txt Section 5.3. The research provides detailed assessment methodologies for different weapon effects (kinetic, directed energy, nuclear) across various orbital regions, with quantitative risk metrics including the Volumetric Collision Rate Index (IVCR). The study details how engagement geometry and space traffic density affect potential damage patterns, with LEO identified as highest risk due to crossing orbits and high satellite density. Effective mitigation strategies and temporal evolution models of collateral effects are included, with cross-references to debris generation sections in I_Astrophysics_Orbital_Mechanics.txt Section 3.3.

- **Thermal Signatures and Heat Dissipation Research** - Detailed analysis of thermal signatures and heat dissipation requirements for space weapons has been integrated into V_Space_Combat_Strategy.txt Section 5.3. The research quantifies specific thermal characteristics of megajoule-class railguns, megawatt-class lasers, and high-thrust missile systems, providing precise heat generation values, spectral distributions, and signature durations. The study examines how these thermal effects impact vessel stealth (increasing detection ranges 3-5×), thermal management design (requiring up to 1,500 m² of radiator area for 1 MW lasers), and tactical firing doctrines (necessitating burst firing patterns with 30-120 second cooling periods). Cross-references to thermal management technologies in IV_Future_Technologies_Engineering.txt Section 3.4 are included.
