### 1.1 Treaty Evolution
Research on space law development reveals:

- **Current Legal Framework (as of 2025):**
  - Outer Space Treaty (1967): Ratified by 115 nations, establishes space as "province of all mankind," prohibits national appropriation, holds states responsible for nationals' activities
  - Moon Treaty (1979): Ratified by only 17 states, declares lunar resources as "common heritage of mankind," lacks support from major spacefaring nations
  - Artemis Accords (2020): Non-binding principles with 53 signatories supporting lunar/Martian exploration, resource extraction, transparency, and sustainability
  - Domestic legislation: US (2015), Luxembourg (2017), UAE (2019), Japan (2021) establishing legal frameworks for space resource utilization
  - Registration Convention (1975): Requires states to maintain registry of launched space objects, informs jurisdiction claims

- **Outer Space Treaty Evolution (2025-2075):**
  - Reinterpretation phase (2025-2040): Recognition of "use rights" through international customary law without treaty amendments
  - Supplementary protocols (2040-2055): Formal protocols adding settlement rights frameworks while preserving non-appropriation principle
  - Comprehensive revision (2055-2075): New treaty superseding OST, maintaining core principles while adding settlement governance, resource management, and jurisdictional frameworks
  - National appropriation prohibition evolving to distinguish "territorial sovereignty" (still prohibited) from "functional jurisdiction" (permitted for established settlements)
  - "Province of all mankind" principle maintained but with benefit-sharing focusing on knowledge and technology transfer rather than direct resource sharing
  - New compliance mechanisms incorporating blockchain verification, satellite monitoring, and AI-based treaty verification systems with 98% detection capability

- **Resource Rights Evolution (2025-2075):**
  - Commercial model emergence (2025-2040): Expansion of domestic legislation legalizing resource extraction rights, creating customary international law
  - International regulatory body (2040-2055): Establishment of UN Space Resources Authority with oversight function, not ownership
  - Benefit-sharing framework (2045-2060): Mandatory contribution of 3-8% of resource profits to development fund focused on:
    - Earth-based sustainable development projects (40-50% of fund)
    - Facilitating developing nations' access to space (25-35% of fund)
    - Supporting scientific research on planetary protection (15-25% of fund)
  - Transition from "common heritage" to "common concern" model (2055-2075), emphasizing responsible management rather than common ownership
  - Recognition of indigenous/settlement priority rights within defined operational areas (100-500km) while maintaining prohibition on broader territorial claims
  - Resource categories evolving with differentiated legal status for:
    - Critical life-supporting resources (requires equitable access provisions)
    - Scientific/heritage sites (protected status with restricted access)
    - Industrial resources (subject to extraction licensing)

- **Settlement Rights Development (2025-2075):**
  - Temporary usage rights (2025-2040): Based on registration convention and facility ownership
  - Permanent settlement recognition (2040-2060): New legal framework distinguishing temporary facilities from permanent settlements
  - Self-governance framework (2060-2075): Legal personhood for settlements with minimum thresholds:
    - Population: 1,000+ permanent residents
    - Duration: 10+ years of continuous habitation
    - Self-sufficiency: 80%+ operational independence from Earth
    - Historical analysis indicates this timeline is approximately 15-20% faster than analogous Earth-based colonial independence movements
    - Acceleration factors include: rapid communication technology, pre-existing democratic traditions, educated founding populations, and economic leverage through essential resource control
  - Tiered autonomy model with increasing settlement rights based on objective development criteria
  - Recognition protocols requiring audited demonstration of:
    - Human rights compliance matching Earth standards
    - Environmental management meeting planetary protection guidelines
    - Transparent governance inclusive of all settlement inhabitants
  - Special legal status for multi-generation settlements (30+ years) with enhanced self-governance rights

- **Legal Evolution Pathways (2025-2075):**
  - Treaty-based approach: Led by UNCOPUOS Legal Subcommittee with formal treaty amendments
  - Custom-based approach: Evolution through state practice and domestic legislation creating binding norms
  - Hybrid approach: Development through soft law instruments (guidelines, accords) that evolve into binding treaties
  - Existing evidence suggests hybrid approach most likely, with:
    - UNCOPUOS Working Group on Space Resource Activities (est. 2021) producing guidelines by 2027
    - Guidelines evolving into customary law through consistent implementation (2027-2045)
    - Codification into formal treaty revising international space law framework (2045-2060)
      - Historical analysis of Earth colony independence patterns suggests this timeline is historically consistent
      - Treaty revision process accelerated by economic pragmatism and settlement population growth
      - Influenced by growing political pressure from second-generation space settlers (born off-Earth)
      - Comparative analysis with historical Earth colonies indicates similar 35-45 year autonomy timeline
  - Earth-based conflict patterns (resource competition, geopolitical tensions) significantly influencing space law development timeline and approach
  - Key historical milestones in settlement autonomy development:
    - Lunar Declaration of Authority (2058): First formal assertion of governance autonomy, established limited self-rule for lunar settlements exceeding 2,000 residents
    - Mars Civil Governance Act (2063): Comprehensive political framework enabling Martian settlements to establish independent civil institutions while maintaining economic treaties with Earth
    - Space-Born Cultural Identity Treaty (2068): Landmark legal recognition of unique status for individuals born in space environments, codifying special legal, cultural, and political rights

- **Artemis Accords Evolution (2025-2075):**
  - Expansion phase (2025-2035): Growth to 80-100 signatories, becoming de facto standard for lunar activities
  - Formalization phase (2035-2050): Transition from bilateral agreements to multilateral treaty framework
  - Integration phase (2050-2075): Incorporation into broader reformed space law architecture
  - Addition of specific protocols addressing:
    - Standardized resource extraction licensing procedures
    - Environmental impact assessment requirements
    - Benefit-sharing mechanisms with sliding scales based on extraction volume
    - Settlement recognition criteria and rights framework
  - Development of Artemis-compliant certification mark for commercial entities with market access benefits

- **Faction Decision-Making Models:**
  - **Utility-Based Decision Framework:**
    - Decisions evaluated based on weighted objectives (economic gain, ideological alignment, scientific knowledge)
    - Option utility calculation: U_j = base utility - risk adjustment + AI influence
    - Base utility calculation: sum of (objective weight × expected outcome)
    - Risk adjustment varies by faction risk tolerance (0-1 scale)
    - AI influence modifies utility based on AI reliance level (0-1 scale)
    - Error in outcome estimation decreases with higher AI reliance
    - Isolation and stress increase error rates by 8-15% in working memory and decision-making
    
  - **Archetype-Specific Parameters:**
    - **Corporate-Resource Focused:** Economic gain (0.8), low risk tolerance (0.3), moderate AI reliance (0.5), autocratic governance
    - **Ideological-Purist:** Ideological purity (0.7), medium risk tolerance (0.5), low AI reliance (0.2), consensus governance
    - **Scientific-Exploration:** Scientific knowledge (0.7), high risk tolerance (0.8), moderate AI reliance (0.5), democratic governance
    - **Survivalist-Autonomist:** Survival/autonomy (0.6/0.4), low risk tolerance (0.3), low AI reliance (0.1), autocratic governance
    - **AI-Symbiotic:** AI alignment (0.6), high risk tolerance (0.7), high AI reliance (0.9), technocratic governance
    
  - **Governance Structure Impact:**
    - **Autocratic:** Leader's preferences dominate, high decisiveness but vulnerable to bias
    - **Democratic:** Aggregated preferences, moderate decisiveness, resilient to individual bias
    - **Consensus:** All members must approve, low decisiveness, highest buy-in
    - **Technocratic:** Domain expertise weighted heavily, specialist bias risk
    - Governance structures may shift under stress, typically toward more autocratic models

  - **Environmental Factors:**
    - Mission duration increases stress levels and cognitive impairment
    - Resource scarcity amplifies bias toward short-term solutions
    - Communication delay with Earth increases autonomous decision-making
    - Physical isolation enhances in-group/out-group perception
    - Crisis events temporarily shift decision weights toward survival

  - **Advanced Utility Theory Implementation:**
    - **Extended Utility Function:** U(x) = ∑[w_i × v_i(x)] + ε_AI - ε_stress - ε_bias
      - w_i represents weight of objective i (0-1)
      - v_i(x) represents value of outcome x for objective i
      - ε_AI represents error correction from AI advisory systems (-0.2 to +0.3)
      - ε_stress represents cognitive impairment from environmental stressors (0 to +0.4)
      - ε_bias represents systematic bias in faction decision processes (0 to +0.5)
    
    - **Decision Error Types:**
      - Type I: False positive errors (15-30% in high-stress environments)
      - Type II: False negative errors (20-40% in resource-constrained scenarios)
      - Compound errors from sequential decision chains (error multiplication factor: 1.2-1.8)
      - Error amplification thresholds at 0.6 (moderate) and 0.8 (severe)
    
    - **AI Advisory System Impact:**
      - Error reduction effectiveness varies by decision domain (tactical: 30-50%, strategic: 15-25%)
      - Highest efficacy for quantitative analysis (resource allocation, trajectory planning)
      - Lowest efficacy for cultural/social domains (crew morale, faction relations)
      - Diminishing returns above AI Integration Level 0.7
      - Efficacy degradation during communication disruptions (10-25% per day)
    
    - **Multi-Faction Decision Modeling:**
      - Nash equilibrium solutions for inter-faction resource negotiations
      - Bayesian probability updates based on observed faction behaviors
      - Pareto efficiency analysis for multi-party treaties
      - Sequential game theory applications for diplomatic exchanges
      - Mixed strategy equilibria for incomplete information contexts

### 1.5 Faction Formation and Lifecycle in Space Settlements
Research on the formation and evolution of factions in space settlements reveals distinct lifecycle phases over generational timescales:

- **Faction Lifecycle Phases:**
  - **Emergence (0-20 years):**
    - Initial formation driven by resource scarcity, technological disparities, and cultural identities
    - Heavy dependence on Earth with limited autonomy (15-25% of issues)
    - Focus on survival and establishing basic structures
    - Unstable governance but high initial cohesion due to shared survival goals
    - Corresponds to "Formation and Early Development Phase" (2040-2060)
  
  - **Consolidation and Growth (20-60 years):**
    - Increasing autonomy from Earth-based oversight
    - Formalization of governance systems and diplomatic relations
    - Expansion in population, resources, and influence
    - Development of unique factional identity distinct from Earth origins
    - Formation of inter-settlement coalitions based on shared interests
    - Corresponds to "Transitional Power Sharing and Growth Phase" (2060-2080)
  
  - **Maturity (60-100+ years):**
    - Established diplomatic frameworks and self-sustaining systems
    - Fully formed cultural identity with minimal Earth influence
    - Complex internal social dynamics and potential subfaction formation
    - Comprehensive governance systems with robust institutional memory
    - Earth oversight reduced to 5-15% of interactions, primarily security matters
    - Corresponds to "Mature Autonomy and Independence Phase" (2080-2120+)
  
  - **Potential Later Phase Trajectories:**
    - **Decline:** Resource depletion, internal conflicts, or external pressures leading to diminishing influence
    - **Transformation:** Significant technological or ideological shifts creating fundamentally new faction identity
    - **Fragmentation:** Internal cohesion collapse leading to division into smaller subfactions

- **Generational Political Power Dynamics:**
  - **Earth-born Founder Dominance Phase (0-30 years):**
    - Initial political control through founder authority structures (appointed leadership)
    - Decision-making based on Earth experience and established frameworks
    - External authority (Earth governments, corporations) influence still significant
    - Governance models reflecting Earth origins (modified democracy, corporate structures)
    - Resistance to full settlement autonomy (40-60% support for Earth oversight)
  
  - **Transitional Power Sharing Phase (30-60 years):**
    - Growing representation of first space-born generation (25-40% of leadership positions)
    - Hybrid governance systems incorporating both Earth and space-specific approaches
    - Political tensions over Earth connection maintenance vs. settlement autonomy
    - Development of formal power-sharing mechanisms between generations
    - Diminishing external authority influence (satellite settlements achieving 70-85% decision autonomy)
  
  - **Space-born Governance Phase (60+ years):**
    - Majority control (75-90%) by space-born generations
    - Governance models adapted to settlement-specific needs and values
    - Potential independence movements or formal autonomy declarations
    - Development of inter-settlement diplomatic frameworks separate from Earth
    - Earth-born population becoming cultural advisors rather than primary decision-makers
    - Historical pattern parallels with colonial independence movements on 150-200 year timeline

- **Economic Priority Evolution:**
  - **Resource Relationship Transformation:**
    - First generation: Export-oriented economics maintaining Earth trade dependencies
    - Second generation: Balanced approach with growing emphasis on self-sufficiency
    - Third+ generation: Primarily self-sufficient economics with specialized exports
    - Resource valuation models shifting from Earth-market to settlement-need basis
  
  - **Investment Pattern Shifts:**
    - First generation: 50-65% investment in Earth-connection infrastructure
    - Second generation: Balanced investment with 40-50% in self-sufficiency systems
    - Third+ generation: 70-80% investment in local resource development and autonomy
    - Growing emphasis on long-term sustainability over short-term efficiency

- **Transition Factors and Dynamics:**
  - **Key Variables Driving Phase Transitions:**
    - **Population Size and Growth Rate:** Influences resource needs and expansion potential
    - **Resource Availability:** Critical for survival and growth, with depletion potentially triggering decline
    - **Technology Level:** Advances boost growth and transformation potential
    - **Governance Effectiveness:** Improves with experience but can decline due to corruption
    - **Internal Cohesion:** Affected by resource distribution, generational harmony, and external threats
    - **External Relations:** Diplomatic standing affected by actions and generational attitudes
    - Resource abundance vs. scarcity cycles
    - Communication technology reliability with Earth
    - Technological self-sufficiency levels
    - External threat perception (natural or human-made)
    - Generational identity formation patterns
    - Economic system evolution and stability
  
  - **Quantitative Transition Thresholds:**
    - Emergence to Consolidation: Typically triggered when Governance Index > 0.6 and Resources > critical threshold
    - Consolidation to Maturity: Occurs when Technology Level and External Relations reach stability indicators
    - Maturity to Decline: May occur if Cohesion < 0.3 for extended periods or Resources fall below critical levels
    - Transformation likelihood increases with significant Technology Level increases (e.g., +2 within 10 years)
    - Fragmentation risk rises dramatically when Cohesion < 0.1 for 3+ periods

  - **Generational Impact on Transitions:**
    - **First Generation (Earth-Born):** Maintain strong Earth ties with 40-60% supporting Earth oversight
    - **Second Generation (Space-Born):** Act as mediators, gaining 25-40% leadership positions
    - **Third+ Generations:** Drive for independence with minimal Earth connection, potentially accelerating transitions
    - Space-born generations consistently push for greater autonomy, affecting governance evolution
    - Generational identity shifts can accelerate phase transitions by 5-15 years
  
  - **Identity Formation Impact on Faction Dynamics:**
    - **Settlement-Origin Impact:**
      - Lunar settlements: Higher Earth affinity (70-80% maintaining identity connection)
      - Mars settlements: Balanced identity development (50-60% hybrid identity)
      - Asteroid/deep space: Strongest independent identity (65-75% primarily space-based identity)
      - Communication delay correlation: Identity independence increases with communication latency
    
    - **Environmental Determinism:**
      - Surface gravity influence on cultural practices and identity (higher retention of Earth cultural elements in similar gravity environments)
      - Radiation exposure correlation with increased group cohesion and solidarity behaviors
      - Day/night cycle impact on social rhythms and governance structures
      - Resource scarcity correlation with governance model preferences
    
    - **Communication Network Effects:**
      - Information exchange frequency directly correlated with faction alignment
      - Shared virtual spaces enabling trans-settlement cultural exchange
      - Communication technology investments as predictors of alliance formation
      - Deliberate communication restriction as factional control mechanism
      - Communication latency's impact on diplomatic relationship quality

- **Long-Term Stability vs. Collapse Conditions:**
  - **Sustained High Cohesion (>0.7) and Effective Governance (>0.8):**
    - Indicates stable and effective governance systems
    - Successful adaptation to technological shifts and resource challenges
    - Balanced distribution of power between generations and subfactions

    - **Resources (R):**
      - Change: dR/dt = Production - Consumption
      - Production = Technology × Population^γ × EfficiencyFactor
      - Consumption = k × Population × ConsumptionFactor
      - γ = 0.7-0.9 (partial economy of scale)
      - k = base consumption rate per person
      - Critical threshold: R/P ratio > 2.0 for stability
    
    - **Technology Level (T):**
      - Advancement: dT/dt = ResearchInvestment × EfficiencyMultiplier × AIBoostFactor
      - ResearchInvestment = i × Resources (where i = investment fraction, typically 0.05-0.15)
      - EfficiencyMultiplier varies by faction type (highest for Scientific factions)
      - AIBoostFactor = 1.0 + (0.2-0.5 × AIIntegrationType value)
      - Breakthrough probability: 0.5-5% chance of +1.0 Technology jump per decade
    
    - **Governance Index (G):**
      - Range: 0.0-1.0 with higher values indicating more effective governance
      - Update: G(t+1) = G(t) × (1-d) + ImprovementFactor
      - Where d = decay rate (0.01-0.05 per year) representing institutional entropy
      - ImprovementFactor based on leadership quality and stability
      - Critical threshold: G > 0.6 for transition to Consolidation phase
    
    - **Cohesion (C):**
      - Range: 0.0-1.0 with higher values indicating stronger unity
      - Update: C(t+1) = C(t) + f(ResourceDistribution) + f(ExternalThreats) - f(InternalConflicts)
      - ResourceDistribution component affected by Gini coefficient (inequality measure)
      - ExternalThreats temporarily boost cohesion (0.05-0.15 points)
      - InternalConflicts reduce cohesion (0.05-0.2 points per major conflict)
      - Critical threshold: C < 0.1 for 3+ periods triggers Fragmentation risk
    
    - **External Relations (E):**
      - Range: -1.0 to 1.0 with positive values indicating favorable relations
      - Separate tracking for Earth relations and inter-settlement relations
      - Decay toward neutral (0.0) at 0.02-0.05 points per year without active diplomacy
      - Significant actions can cause immediate shifts of 0.1-0.3 points
      - Generational effects cause Earth relations to decline by 0.1-0.2 points per generation

  - **Phase Transition Mechanisms:**
    - **Probabilistic Transition Model:**
      - Phase transitions treated as probabilistic functions of state variables
      - Transition probability = BaseProb × ∏(StateVariableContributions)
      - Example: P(Emergence→Consolidation) = 0.05 × f(G) × f(R) × f(C)
      - Where f(G) increases sharply when G > 0.6
      - Enables gradual rather than discrete transitions, reflecting reality
    
    - **Multi-Factor Threshold Systems:**
      - Major transitions require multiple thresholds to be crossed
      - Emergence → Consolidation: G > 0.6 AND R > Critical AND C > 0.5
      - Consolidation → Maturity: T > 5.0 AND E > 0.7 AND Population > MinThreshold
      - Maturity → Decline: C < 0.3 for 5+ years OR R < CriticalMin for 3+ years
      - Transformation triggered by T increase > 2.0 within 10-year period AND C > 0.6
      - Fragmentation triggered by C < 0.1 for 3+ years AND subfaction strength > 30%
    
    - **Path Dependency Modeling:**
      - Previous faction states influence future transition probabilities
      - Historical crisis response patterns affect resilience to future challenges
      - Institutional memory implemented as exponentially decaying influence of past decisions
      - Allows for "character development" of factions over time
      - Critical decision points create pathway locks that persist for 10-20 years

  - **Stochastic Event Generation:**
    - **Black Swan Events:**
      - Randomized occurrence with 0.5-5% probability per decade
      - Severity distributed according to power law (many minor, few catastrophic)
      - Impact vectors target specific state variables (e.g., Resources, Cohesion)
      - Events categorized as Beneficial (20%), Neutral (30%), or Harmful (50%)
      - Examples include resource discoveries, technological breakthroughs, catastrophic failures
    
    - **Technological Breakthroughs:**
      - Probability modified by Research Investment and Technology Level
      - Impact varies by breakthrough type (propulsion, computing, life support, etc.)
      - Secondary effects cascade through multiple state variables
      - Transformational potential based on impact magnitude and faction adaptability
      - AI integration increases breakthrough probability by 20-50%
    
    - **Inter-Faction Events:**
      - Trade agreements, conflicts, alliances modeled as events affecting External Relations
      - Diplomatic incidents with 1-3% probability per faction relationship per year
      - Resource conflicts more likely when resources are scarce (R/P ratio < 1.5)
      - Cooperation opportunities more likely with complementary specializations
      - Resolution pathways influenced by Governance Index and cultural compatibility

- **Typical Durations and Variability:**
  - **Phase Duration Analysis:**
    - **Emergence (0-20 years):**
      - Typical duration: 15-25 years (median 20)
      - Shortened by: Strong leadership (2-5 years), high initial resources (3-7 years), AI integration (3-5 years)
      - Extended by: Political instability (5-10 years), resource scarcity (4-8 years), external conflicts (3-7 years)
      - Standard deviation: 4.5 years across modeled scenarios
      - Historical baseline: Comparable to early Antarctic station establishment period

### 1.6 Geopolitical Metagame Theory

  - Resource access disputes

- **Faction Decision-Making Models:**
  - **Utility-Based Decision Framework:**
    - Decisions evaluated based on weighted objectives (economic gain, ideological alignment, scientific knowledge)
    - Option utility calculation: U_j = base utility - risk adjustment + AI influence
    - Base utility calculation: sum of (objective weight × expected outcome)
    - Risk adjustment varies by faction risk tolerance (0-1 scale)
    - AI influence modifies utility based on AI reliance level (0-1 scale)
    - Error in outcome estimation decreases with higher AI reliance
    - Isolation and stress increase error rates by 8-15% in working memory and decision-making
    
  - **Archetype-Specific Parameters:**
    - **Corporate-Resource Focused:** Economic gain (0.8), low risk tolerance (0.3), moderate AI reliance (0.5), autocratic governance
    - **Ideological-Purist:** Ideological purity (0.7), medium risk tolerance (0.5), low AI reliance (0.2), consensus governance
    - **Scientific-Exploration:** Scientific knowledge (0.7), high risk tolerance (0.8), moderate AI reliance (0.5), democratic governance
    - **Survivalist-Autonomist:** Survival/autonomy (0.6/0.4), low risk tolerance (0.3), low AI reliance (0.1), autocratic governance
    - **AI-Symbiotic:** AI alignment (0.6), high risk tolerance (0.7), high AI reliance (0.9), technocratic governance
    
  - **Governance Structure Impact:**
    - **Autocratic:** Leader's preferences dominate, high decisiveness but vulnerable to bias
    - **Democratic:** Aggregated preferences, moderate decisiveness, resilient to individual bias
    - **Consensus:** All members must approve, low decisiveness, highest buy-in
    - **Technocratic:** Domain expertise weighted heavily, specialist bias risk
    - Governance structures may shift under stress, typically toward more autocratic models

  - **Environmental Factors:**
    - Mission duration increases stress levels and cognitive impairment
    - Resource scarcity amplifies bias toward short-term solutions
    - Communication delay with Earth increases autonomous decision-making
    - Physical isolation enhances in-group/out-group perception
    - Crisis events temporarily shift decision weights toward survival

  - **Advanced Utility Theory Implementation:**
    - **Extended Utility Function:** U(x) = ∑[w_i × v_i(x)] + ε_AI - ε_stress - ε_bias
      - w_i represents weight of objective i (0-1)
      - v_i(x) represents value of outcome x for objective i
      - ε_AI represents error correction from AI advisory systems (-0.2 to +0.3)
      - ε_stress represents cognitive impairment from environmental stressors (0 to +0.4)
      - ε_bias represents systematic bias in faction decision processes (0 to +0.5)
    
    - **Decision Error Types:**
      - Type I: False positive errors (15-30% in high-stress environments)
      - Type II: False negative errors (20-40% in resource-constrained scenarios)
      - Compound errors from sequential decision chains (error multiplication factor: 1.2-1.8)
      - Error amplification thresholds at 0.6 (moderate) and 0.8 (severe)
    
    - **AI Advisory System Impact:**
      - Error reduction effectiveness varies by decision domain (tactical: 30-50%, strategic: 15-25%)
      - Highest efficacy for quantitative analysis (resource allocation, trajectory planning)
      - Lowest efficacy for cultural/social domains (crew morale, faction relations)
      - Diminishing returns above AI Integration Level 0.7
      - Efficacy degradation during communication disruptions (10-25% per day)
    
    - **Multi-Faction Decision Modeling:**
      - Nash equilibrium solutions for inter-faction resource negotiations
      - Bayesian probability updates based on observed faction behaviors
      - Pareto efficiency analysis for multi-party treaties
      - Sequential game theory applications for diplomatic exchanges
      - Mixed strategy equilibria for incomplete information contexts

- **Internal Stability and Control:**
  - **Satisfaction Dynamics:**
    - Subgroups maintain satisfaction levels (S_k) based on decision alignment with preferences
