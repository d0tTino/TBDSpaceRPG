## 1.0 Propulsion Systems
Research on propulsion informs the performance characteristics of spacecraft and mission planning.

### 1.1 Advanced Chemical
Chemical propulsion remains the mainstay for launch vehicles and high-thrust maneuvers. Research continues to improve:

#### Efficiency improvements
- Projected ISP increase from current ~450s to theoretical maximum of 480-500s by 2080 through:
  - Advanced catalyst materials increasing reaction efficiency
  - Combustion chamber geometry optimization
  - Nozzle design improvements for better expansion ratio management
  - Propellant pre-heating systems and energy recovery

#### New propellant combinations
- Metal-doped propellants showing 5-10% performance improvement
- Solid-core hybrids with throttling capabilities
- Semi-cryogenic combinations with storage advantages
- Green propellants with reduced toxicity and handling requirements

#### Manufacturability in space
- In-situ production viability metrics:
  - Oxygen extraction from regolith with 60-70% efficiency (2050)
  - Aluminum/regolith-derived propellant varieties by 2080
  - Water-derived H2/O2 from NEO or lunar ice with 80% process efficiency

## 3.0 Communication Systems
Research on communication systems informs mission constraints and gameplay mechanics related to information flow.

### 3.1 Interplanetary Communications

#### 3.1.1 Latency and Bandwidth Constraints

Signal latency remains a fundamental physical constraint for interplanetary operations:
- Earth-Mars: 4-24 minutes depending on orbital alignment
- Earth-Jupiter: 35-52 minutes 
- Inner-Outer Solar System: Up to 8 hours

#### Bandwidth projections:
- Near-term (2030-2050): 
  - Mars-Earth: 10-100 Mbps
  - Jupiter-Earth: 1-10 Mbps
- Mid-term (2050-2100):
  - Mars-Earth: 1-10 Gbps
  - Jupiter-Earth: 100-500 Mbps
- Long-term (2100-2150):
  - Mars-Earth: 50-100 Gbps
  - Jupiter-Earth: 1-5 Gbps

Bandwidth will remain concentrated at designated communication nodes and habitats, with temporary settlements and exploration teams having significantly lower capacity.

#### 3.1.2 Optical Communication Advancements

Research into deep-space optical communications suggests:
- 10-100x bandwidth improvements over comparable RF systems
- Power requirements of 30-50% less than equivalent RF systems
- Pointing accuracy requirements of <1 μrad for reliable operation
- Development of hybrid optical/RF systems for weather resilience

Projected operational capabilities:
- 2040: 1 Gbps from Mars orbit with 50cm apertures
- 2060: 10 Gbps from Jupiter orbit with 1m apertures
- 2100: 100 Gbps from Saturn orbit with 2m apertures

### 3.2 Delay-Tolerant Networking

The evolution of delay-tolerant networking will define information transfer capabilities:

#### Store-and-forward infrastructure:
- Data custody transfer protocols to prevent loss
- Opportunistic routing based on movement predictions 
- Prioritization schemas for critical vs. non-critical data
- Dynamic compression ratios based on:
  - Content type
  - Available bandwidth
  - Transmission urgency
  - Power constraints

#### Independent operation capabilities:
- Local processing autonomy requiring:
  - 90-99% reduction in Earth consultations for routine operations
  - AI-powered decision support systems
  - Pre-positioned knowledge bases with multi-layered access

#### System architecture evolution:
- 2030-2050: Centralized Earth-based authority with local caching
- 2050-2080: Federated model with Earth as primary authority, local autonomy for time-sensitive operations
- 2080-2150: Distributed authority model with:
  - Local deterministic operation
  - Asynchronous consensus protocols
  - Minimal Earth dependencies for routine operations
